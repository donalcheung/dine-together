- # TableMesh Launch Week: Video Scripts & Prompts\n\nThis document contains video scripts for the first week of TableMesh\'s launch on TikTok and YouTube, along with creative prompts for generating supplementary visuals using Hedra and Seedance.\n\n---\n\n## Day 1: App Launch Announcement (TikTok)\n\n- **Concept:** Founder-led announcement, showing the passion and problem behind the app.\n- **Format:** Fast-paced cuts between the founder talking, screen recordings of the app, and lifestyle shots.\n- **Hook:** "I spent the last year building an app to solve my own problem..."\n\n**Script:**\n\n**(Scene: Founder in a casual setting, maybe their home office)**\n\n**Founder:** (To camera) "I spent the last year building an app to solve my own problem: I was tired of eating alone."\n\n**(Quick cut to screen recording of scrolling through a sad-looking food delivery app)**\n\n**VO:** "I love trying new restaurants, but so many of the best meals are meant to be shared."\n\n**(Cut back to Founder)**\n\n**Founder:** "And planning group dinners with friends? It\'s a nightmare of group chats and scheduling conflicts."\n\n**(Quick cut to the "Group Chat Chaos" thumbnail image)**\n\n**VO:** "So I built TableMesh."\n\n**(Cut to a fast-paced montage of the app\'s key features: creating a dining request, discovering other requests, the chat interface)**\n\n**VO:** "It\'s an app that connects you with new people for shared dining experiences. Whether you\'re new to a city, your friends are busy, or you just want to expand your social circle."\n\n**(Cut back to Founder, holding phone with app open)**\n\n**Founder:** "It\'s finally live. If you\'ve ever felt the same way, download it now and let\'s share a meal."\n\n**On-screen text:** Download TableMesh. Link in bio!\n\n---\n\n## Day 2: What is TableMesh? (YouTube Explainer)\n\n- **Concept:** A clear, concise explainer video that walks through the app\'s value proposition.\n- **Format:** Animated text, screen recordings, and stock footage.\n- **Hook:** "This is for anyone who eats alone most nights."\n\n**Script:**\n\n**(Scene: Upbeat, animated intro with the TableMesh logo)**\n\n**VO:** "Are you tired of eating alone? Do you love food but hate the hassle of organizing group dinners?"\n\n**(Visual: Split screen showing a person eating alone vs. a happy group eating together)**\n\n**VO:** "Introducing TableMesh, the app that brings people together over a shared love of food."\n\n**(Visual: Animated text showing the three core features)**\n\n**VO:** "With TableMesh, you can:\n1.  **Discover & Join:** Find dining requests from people in your city.\n2.  **Host Your Own:** Create your own dining events for others to join.\n3.  **Connect & Chat:** Message other users and coordinate your plans securely within the app."\n\n**(Visual: Screen recording of a user creating a dining request, setting the restaurant, time, and number of guests)**\n\n**VO:** "It\'s simple. Just pick a restaurant, set a time, and let people join you. Or, browse what\'s happening around you and join a table."\n\n**(Visual: Montage of diverse groups of people laughing and eating at different types of restaurants)**\n\n**VO:** "Stop eating alone. Stop the endless group chats. Start sharing meals and making new friends."\n\n**(Visual: End screen with app logo and download buttons for App Store and Google Play)**\n\n**VO:** "Download TableMesh today. Your next favorite meal is waiting."\n\n---\n\n## Day 3: Group Chat Chaos Skit (TikTok)\n\n- **Concept:** A relatable, humorous skit about the pain of planning a group dinner.\n- **Format:** A single person playing multiple characters, using trending audio.\n- **Hook:** "POV: You\'re trying to plan a group dinner"\n\n**Script:**\n\n**(Scene: A person looking increasingly stressed while texting on their phone. Use a popular, chaotic-sounding trending audio in the background.)**\n\n**On-screen text captions appearing rapidly:**\n\n*   "Friend 1: I can\'t do Friday."\n*   "Friend 2: What about that new sushi place?"\n*   "Friend 3: I\'m vegetarian \uD83E\uDD66"\n*   "Friend 1: Can we do 7pm instead of 8?"\n*   "Friend 4: Who\'s driving?"\n*   "Friend 2: I\'m allergic to shellfish."\n*   "Friend 3: I don\'t like sushi."\n\n**(The person finally looks up at the camera with a deadpan expression. The music stops.)**\n\n**On-screen text:** There has to be a better way.\n\n**(Cut to a screen recording of the TableMesh app, showing a clean, simple dining request.)**\n\n**On-screen text:** TableMesh. Group dining, simplified. Link in bio.\n\n---\n\n## Hedra/Seedance Prompts\n\nThese prompts can be used to generate abstract, visually interesting clips to use as backgrounds or quick cuts in the videos.\n\n- **Prompt 1 (Vibrant Social Energy):** "A kaleidoscope of colorful, abstract shapes and lights moving in a fluid, energetic motion. The feeling of laughter and conversation visualized. Warm tones of gold, orange, and red, with flashes of bright color. A sense of joyful, social connection. Cinematic, bokeh lighting."\n\n- **Prompt 2 (The Art of Food):** "An extreme close-up, slow-motion shot of steam rising from a beautifully plated dish. Macro photography style. The textures of the food are hyper-detailed. The movement is graceful and mesmerizing. The colors are rich and appetizing. A celebration of culinary art."\n\n- **Prompt 3 (Connecting Paths):** "An abstract visualization of glowing lines and particles flowing and connecting in a dark space. The lines represent individual paths, which converge to form a vibrant, interconnected network. The feeling of separate journeys meeting to create a community. A mix of cool blues and warm yellows."\n
