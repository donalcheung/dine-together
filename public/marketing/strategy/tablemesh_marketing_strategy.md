# TableMesh TikTok & YouTube Marketing Strategy

**Prepared by:** Manus AI
**Date:** March 7, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive marketing strategy for TableMesh on TikTok and YouTube. The goal is to drive app downloads, build brand awareness, and create a community around social dining. The strategy focuses on authentic, relatable content that addresses the core problems TableMesh solves: the friction of organizing group meals and the loneliness of solo dining.

## 2. Target Audience

Our content will target four key audience segments:

| Segment | Description | Pain Points | Content Hooks |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Organizer** | The friend who always plans group outings | Group chat chaos, finding a time that works, managing RSVPs | "Stop herding cats in group chat," "The best way to plan a group dinner" |
| **The Solo Diner** | New to the city, remote worker, or just wants to meet new people | Eating alone, missing out on shared dining experiences, social anxiety | "If you eat alone most nights, stop scrolling," "How to meet new people in your city" |
| **The Foodie** | Loves trying new restaurants and cuisines | Can't try family-style dishes alone, wants to share food experiences | "The best Korean BBQ is meant to be shared," "I built an app to find people to try new restaurants with" |
| **The Team Lead** | Organizes team lunches and happy hours | Finding a place everyone likes, managing dietary restrictions, awkward team lunches | "Your team lunches are awkward because...", "How to plan a team lunch that isn't boring" |

## 3. Content Pillars

Our content will revolve around five core pillars:

| Pillar | Description | Sample Content Ideas |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem/Solution** | Relatable skits and stories that highlight the pain points of solo dining and group coordination, then present TableMesh as the solution. | - POV: You're trying to plan a group dinner in a 20-message group chat
- Before/after: Eating alone vs. sharing a meal with new friends
- "I built an app to solve my own problem of eating alone" |
| **Restaurant & Food** | Showcase amazing restaurants and dishes that are best enjoyed in a group. | - "3 restaurants in [City] you have to try with a group"
- "This Korean BBQ spot is a must-try for your next team lunch"
- "The best family-style Italian in [City]" |
| **Community & Stories** | Share real or hypothetical stories of people connecting over meals. | - "I met my new best friend at a TableMesh dinner"
- "From strangers to friends: the story of our first TableMesh meal"
- "What happens when you have dinner with 5 strangers" |
| **Founder & BTS** | Authentic, behind-the-scenes content about building the app. | - "Day in the life of an app founder"
- "Fixing a critical bug at 2 AM"
- "Reading user feedback and planning new features" |
| **How-To & Guides** | Practical tips and guides related to social dining. | - "How to host a dinner party for people you've never met"
- "3 conversation starters for dining with new people"
- "The introvert's guide to social dining" |

## 4. Platform Strategy

### TikTok
- **Frequency:** 3-5 videos per week
- **Format:** Short-form (15-30 seconds), trending audio, fast cuts, text overlays
- **Focus:** Relatable skits, POV videos, food content, founder BTS
- **CTA:** "Download TableMesh (link in bio)"

### YouTube
- **Frequency:** 2-3 Shorts per week (repurpose best TikToks), 1 long-form video per month
- **Format (Shorts):** Same as TikTok
- **Format (Long-form):** Deeper dives (10-15 min) into topics like "The Loneliness Epidemic," "How We Built TableMesh," or city-specific food guides.
- **Focus:** Building a more engaged community, providing more value, driving SEO
- **CTA:** "Download TableMesh (link in description)"
