
# TableMesh Social Media Launch Kit

**Platform Focus:** Instagram & TikTok
**Target Audience:** Urban millennials & Gen Z (22-35), foodies, social connectors, young professionals.
**Brand Voice:** Energetic, friendly, witty, and passionate about food and connection.

---

## Content Pillars

1.  **The Problem (Relatability):** Posts highlighting the pain of coordinating group meals.
    *   *Examples:* Chaotic group chat screenshots, polls ("How many messages does it take to pick a restaurant?"), memes about analysis paralysis.
2.  **The Solution (App Features):** Showcasing how TableMesh solves these problems.
    *   *Examples:* Screen recordings of hosting a table in 30 seconds, tutorials on the share link, highlighting the RSVP tracking.
3.  **The Experience (User-Generated Content & Stories):** Celebrating successful meals and connections.
    *   *Examples:* Reposting user photos from TableMesh dinners (with permission), sharing testimonials, "Table of the Week" spotlights.
4.  **The Food (Local Discovery):** Partnering with local food bloggers and highlighting amazing restaurants.
    *   *Examples:* "Best spots for Korean BBQ in [City]", "3 restaurants you have to try this weekend", influencer takeovers.
5.  **The Mission (Brand Building):** Reinforcing the "why" behind TableMesh.
    *   *Examples:* Posts about the loneliness epidemic, the joy of shared meals, founder stories.

## Sample Posts (Launch Week)

### Post 1: The Problem (Reel/TikTok)

*   **Video:** A fast-paced, sped-up video of someone scrolling endlessly through a chaotic group chat about dinner plans. Text overlays pop up: "Where to eat?" "Not there." "I can't do 7." "What about..."
*   **Audio:** A trending, slightly chaotic or frustrated-sounding audio clip.
*   **Caption:** It shouldn't take 47 messages to pick a restaurant. End the group chat chaos. Link in bio. #tablemesh #groupdinner #wheretoeat #[LaunchCity]food

### Post 2: The Solution (Carousel)

*   **Slide 1 (Image):** A beautiful, high-quality photo of a delicious-looking dish with the text: "Your group chat could never."
