## The Problem: A Social Recession Fueled by Solo Dining

Americans are dining out more than ever, with average household spending on restaurants reaching $371 per month in 2025, a 30% increase from 2019 [1]. Yet, a concerning trend has emerged: people are increasingly eating alone. One in four Americans now eats all their meals in solitude, a 53% increase since 2003 [2]. This "loneliness epidemic" is particularly acute among young adults, where dining alone has surged by 80% [3]. The core issue is not a lack of desire for social connection, but the high-friction process of coordinating group meals. Existing tools like group chats are chaotic and lack dining-specific features, while platforms like Meetup are too broad and platforms like Timeleft remove user choice.

## The Solution: A Purpose-Built Group Dining Coordination Tool

TableMesh is a mobile-first platform that makes organizing group meals effortless. It is not another app for meeting strangers; it is a practical utility for coordinating meals with the people already in your life—friends, coworkers, and family—while also enabling the discovery of new dining companions in a safe, public setting. Users can host a table in under 30 seconds, share a link that works for non-app users, and manage RSVPs and communication in one place.

## Market Opportunity: A $6.6B+ Market at the Intersection of Social & Tech

TableMesh operates at the convergence of three large and growing markets:

*   **Social Dining Platforms:** A $6.64 billion market in 2025, growing steadily [4].
*   **Group Dining Platforms:** A niche projected to grow 5x from $442.8 million in 2025 to $2.27 billion by 2035 [5].
*   **Restaurant Technology:** A massive $59.3 billion market in 2024, projected to reach $314.85 billion by 2033 (16.4% CAGR) [6].

By solving the coordination problem, TableMesh unlocks higher spending from group diners (who spend 2-3x more than solo diners) for restaurants and provides a much-needed social outlet for consumers.

## Business Model: Dual Revenue Streams

1.  **Consumer Subscriptions (B2C):** A freemium model, "TableMesh Pro," offers premium features like advanced filters, request boosts, and unlimited deals for a monthly fee. This is powered by RevenueCat for seamless cross-platform implementation.
2.  **Restaurant SaaS (B2B):** A tiered subscription for restaurants to access the platform's group dining audience. The "Pro" plan ($49/month) allows restaurants to post deals, access analytics, and gain promoted placement. This is powered by Stripe.

## Traction & Growth Strategy

With zero marketing spend, the platform has already seen organic adoption, with **31 users** creating **33 dining requests** and **21 joins**. Our growth strategy is viral by design:

1.  **City-by-City Launch:** Seed the platform in a target metro area with foodie and coworker groups.
2.  **Viral Sharing Loop:** Every shared meal link is an acquisition channel. Non-app users can view details and RSVP via the web, creating a powerful funnel to app downloads.
3.  **Restaurant Partnerships:** Onboard local restaurants with a free tier to populate the platform with deals, creating a flywheel effect that attracts more diners.
